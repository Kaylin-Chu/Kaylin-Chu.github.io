What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

On a very basic level;
  • Border is the ring around the outside of the content (element/text)
  • Padding is the space between the text and the border
  • Margin is the space around the border

Shown here, the Box model is a visual representation of how the Border, Padding and Margins work.
It looks a bit like a square target, the content is the bullseye, then the outer ring is the padding, then the border and lastly, the margin.

Content > Padding > Border > Margin

One way to remember the difference is to use Will McGugan's mnemonic, 'padded cell'. In a padded cell, the padding goes on the inside.

When to use Margins, Borders, and Padding?

Borders are used to outline or "circle" content, if the margin lines are too close to the content/text you would add in padding to make the border ring further away. Margins are used if the next element is too close to the border, this will push it further away.

Other things to note:

  • Thickening the borders will change the element size. To outline without changing the element size, use the "outline" command.
  • Vertical margins overlap, the largest will engulf the smaller ones rather than being added together
  • Unlike vertical margins, Horizontal margins will be added together
