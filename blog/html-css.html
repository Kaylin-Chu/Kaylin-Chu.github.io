What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

On a very basic level;
  • Border is the ring around the outside of the text (element/content)
  • Padding is the space between the text and the border
  • Margin is the space around the border

It looks a bit like a square target, the text is the bullseye then the outer ring is the padding then the border and then the margin.

Text > Padding > Border > Margin

One way to tell the difference is to use Will McGugan's mnemonic, 'padded cell'. In a padded cell, the padding goes on the inside.

Other things to note:

  • Thickening the borders will change the element size. To outline without changing the element size, use the "outline" command.
  • Vertical margins overlap, the largest will engulf the smaller ones rather than being added together
  • Unlike vertical margins, Horizontal margins will be added together
  • Borders make margins non-collapsible
